Meet Our Team

The people who make InspireU what it is: experienced, compassionate, and genuinely committed to making a difference.

The InspireU team

People you can count on

Our team brings together experience, heart, and a shared passion for making a genuine difference every single day. Trust starts with knowing who is in your corner.

Kyle Schneider

His background in Rugby League shapes his approach to teamwork, resilience, and discipline, qualities that translate directly into his care. Kyle's commitment to growth, both on and off the field, reflects his ability to inspire confidence and build genuine connections with those he supports.

Bailee Sulley

Bailee is known for her compassionate nature and steady reliability. Her adaptability and patience make her a trusted support worker who consistently delivers high-quality care. Driven by a strong work ethic and a genuine desire to help others, Bailee creates meaningful relationships that empower participants to thrive. Outside of work, she enjoys netball, family time, and adventures with her dog.

Bailey Schneider

📍 Central Coast Support Worker

Bailey's passion for the disability sector shines through in everything he does. His focus on inclusion and empowerment helps participants reach their full potential. Rae Wakeham

📍 Central Coast Senior Support Worker

With 24 years of experience in the support sector, Rae brings deep understanding and empathy to her role. A proud mother of two, she combines strength and compassion to help others live life to the fullest. Rae's participants value her patience, warmth, and genuine care. When she's not working, Rae enjoys touch football and spending time with family and friends.

Joseph Ransfield (RN)

📍 Cairns Registered Nurse

Originally from New Zealand, Joseph is a registered nurse with 24 years of experience and a holistic approach to care. His humour and empathy create a positive, reassuring environment for participants. Joseph's cultural tattoo, his Mataora, represents his life journey and connection to family and tribe, a reflection of the respect and authenticity he brings to his work. Outside of nursing, he loves fishing, cooking, and cheering for the Bulldogs and NSW Blues alongside his wife and eight children.
